🤔 What is the difference between the two?

“Raining right now” = weather — what is happening TODAY. “Hot all year” = climate — the average pattern over MANY years. Keep that rule in your pocket; you will use It all lesson!

Section 2 · Learn it

⏱️ Today vs. Many Years

Weather is what happens TODAY — this morning's rain, this afternoon's wind. Climate is the average pattern over MANY years — Belize is hot and humid year-round.

Two measurements decide a place's climate type: temperature 🌡️ and precipitation (rainfall) 🌧️.

Weather = now. Climate = the long-run pattern — and It decides a place's climate type.

🌴 Belize sits in the tropical band: hot all year, with a dry season (roughly February–May) and a wet (rainy) season (roughly June–November).

🗽 One comparison to remember: Belize City and New York City have different climates because their seasonal changes differ — Belize's are wet and dry, while New York swings from hot to cold.

Pocket rule: weather = now / today · climate = the long-run average. One cold morning does NOT change Belize's tropical climate.
