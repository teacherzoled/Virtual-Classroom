🌦️ Weather vs Climate

One minute to refresh. Then the practice begins.

Weather is what the air is doing today or right now — this morning's rain, this afternoon's wind. Climate is the average pattern of weather in a place over many years.

Two measurements decide a place's climate type: temperature (how hot or cold) and precipitation (rain and other water that falls). Here is the picture Mr. EdLo drew on the board:

Weather is now; climate is the long-run average. Temperature and precipitation name the climate type.

🌴 Belize's climate type is tropical: hot all year, with a wet season and a dry season instead of a hot and a cold one.

🌏 Different places, different climates. Belize City swings from wet to dry; New York City swings from hot to cold. Same two measures, different patterns — so different climate types.

Belize sits near the equator, so it is warm every month, and its low coastline and reef mean the wet and dry seasons matter for farming and fishing. That is why knowing our own climate matters here at home.

☔ WEATHER — what the air is doing this moment: today's rain, today's wind, today's heat.
📅 CLIMATE — the average over many years: Belize is tropical, with a wet and a dry season.

One cold morning does not change Belize's climate. Weather is now; climate is the long run.
