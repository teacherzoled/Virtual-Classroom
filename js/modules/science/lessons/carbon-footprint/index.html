What is a Carbon Footprint? ü§î

Imagine you leave a footprint in the sand. Our actions also leave a mark on the Earth, but it's invisible! This is our carbon footprint.

It's the total amount of greenhouse gases (like carbon dioxide) that are created by our everyday activities. These gases trap heat in the atmosphere and cause the Earth to get warmer, which is called global warming.

The biggest parts of our footprint usually come from:

  • Transportation: How we get around (cars, buses).
  • Housing: The electricity we use at home.
  • Food: The energy it takes to grow and transport our food.

How Do Our Footprints Affect Belize? üáßüáø

A bigger carbon footprint means a warmer planet. For Belize, this is a big deal! It can cause:

  • Rising Sea Levels: This can cause more flooding in places like Belize City and other coastal areas.
  • Coral Reef Damage: Our beautiful barrier reef can get sick and lose its color (coral bleaching) when the water gets too warm.
  • Extreme Weather: We might see stronger hurricanes and storms.
  • Farming Troubles: It can become harder for farmers to grow the food we love to eat.
