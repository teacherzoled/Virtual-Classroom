Step 2 📖 Scripture Study — Romans 5:8 +20 XP 10 min
🔒 Complete Step 1 to unlock.
Romans 5:8 (KJV)
"But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us."
📌 Key: God acted first — before anyone repented, believed, or deserved it.

Study the verse carefully, then answer:

In your own words, what does Romans 5:8 tell us about when God showed His love?
Model Answer
Romans 5:8 tells us that God showed His love while we were still sinners — not after we changed or deserved it. He did not wait for people to be good. Christ died for sinners first, which shows that God takes the initiative in salvation.
Why is it important that God loved us before we deserved it?
Model Answer
It shows that salvation is not based on human effort or worthiness. No one could earn God's love, so it is completely a gift — grace. This means everyone has the opportunity to receive it, not only people who seem "good enough."

Step 4 💬 Guided Discussion +20 XP 8 min
🔒 Complete Step 3 to unlock.

Write a complete-sentence answer for each question.

Why does salvation begin with God and not with people? Use Romans 5:8 to support your answer.
Model Answer
Salvation begins with God because He loved us and sent Christ to die for us while we were still sinners (Romans 5:8). No one chose God first — God chose to act first. People cannot save themselves; it is God's grace that makes salvation possible.
How does grace show God's love? Give a specific example.
Model Answer
Grace shows God's love because it is given freely to people who do not deserve it. A specific example is that God sent His Son to die for sinners (Romans 5:8), not waiting for them to become worthy. This gift — which no one can earn — is the clearest proof of how much God loves people.

Step 2 📖 John 6:44 & Acts 16:14 — The Father Draws +25 XP 10 min
🔒 Complete Step 1 to unlock.
John 6:44 (KJV)
"No man can come to me, except the Father which hath sent me draw him: and I will raise him up at the last day."
📌 The word "draw" means the Father actively pulls people toward Jesus. This is prevenient grace — God acts first.
Acts 16:14 (KJV)
"And a certain woman named Lydia… heard us: whose heart the Lord opened, that she attended unto the things which were spoken of Paul."
📌 Lydia's heart was opened by the Lord — she did not open it herself. God's grace worked in her before she responded.
In John 6:44, what does it mean that the Father "draws" people? Explain in your own words.
Model Answer
When the Father "draws" people, it means He actively works in their hearts and brings them toward Jesus. It is not something the person does alone — God creates the desire, the awareness, and the pull toward Jesus before the person even makes a decision.
How does the story of Lydia (Acts 16:14) show prevenient grace? Who acted first — Lydia or God?
Model Answer
The story of Lydia shows prevenient grace because God acted first — the Lord opened her heart before she responded. Lydia did not open her own heart. God prepared her heart to receive the message, and then she listened and believed. This shows that God's grace comes before human response.

Step 2 📖 Titus 2:11 — Grace Appears and Teaches +20 XP 8 min
🔒 Complete Step 1 to unlock.
Titus 2:11 (KJV)
"For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men, teaching us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world."
📌 Grace does two things: it brings salvation (God's initiative) and teaches us how to respond (our response).
According to Titus 2:11, what does grace "teach" people to do?
Model Answer
According to Titus 2:11, grace teaches people to say no to ungodliness and worldly desires, and to live soberly, righteously, and godly in this world. Grace is not only a gift — it actively shapes how people live and respond to God.
